# Document Tab Management

> 💡**When to use this:**
> When your study set has too many documents to fit in the tab bar;
> When you want to hide documents you are not currently studying to reduce distraction;
> When you want to keep frequently used documents at the front of the tab bar.

> ❗This page is based on the tab feature. Beyond tab management, there are other advanced ways to use it. For details, see the page New Blank Notebook, Comparison ①: Bi-article Comparison Window.

## 1 What Are Document Tabs?

A **study set** contains all the documents you have imported, while the **tab bar** only shows the documents you choose to open.

Just like browser tabs, each tab corresponds to one open document, letting you switch between them quickly.

![Document tab bar](image/9c31b1a7cf14b982.webp)

**Showing and hiding the tab bar**:

Tap `Document - More` (the vertical three-dot icon) in the top-right corner → `Show Document Tabs` to show or hide the tab bar.

![Showing and hiding the tab bar](image/55683e2d2dd1fc24.webp)

## 2 Working with Document Tabs

### 2.1 Browsing and Opening Documents

Tap the document list button on the right to open the document list.

- On this screen, you can quickly browse every document in the study set.
- Tap a document to open it instantly in the tab bar.

![Tap the expand icon on the right](image/ca6dbe66ae3d22a2.webp)

![Tap any document in the document list to open it in a tab](image/44efd77c22f0336a.webp)

### 2.2 Closing Documents

Tap the **x** on the right side of a tab to close that tab's document.

![Tap the x on the right side of the tab](image/f88fab2fcaaddd0d.webp)

### 2.3 Importing Documents

Tap the `➕` on the right side of the tab bar to **import a document** into the study set; the imported document opens automatically in the tab bar.

For the detailed steps, see: [Importing Documents into a Study Set](../vtjSQ6LvQv1TbbjdctzroM/index.md)

![Tap the plus sign to import a document](image/f6d4e881f2e954a4.webp)

## 3 Managing Document Tabs

Tap `Manage` to go to the tab management screen.

![Tap Manage](image/44efd77c22f0336a.webp)

### 3.1 Closing a Document from a Tab

In the `Documents in Tabs` section, tap the **x** on the right side of a tab to close that tab's document. You can also close it directly in the tab bar; see: Closing Documents.

![](image/a35ac60e87cf61df.webp)

> ❗Closing a tab does not delete the document. You can reopen it at any time from the documents in your study set (see: Browsing and Opening Documents).

### 3.2 Removing a Document from a Study Set

As shown below, tap the `⛔️` on the tab management screen to remove the document from the `study set`.

![Closing a document from the study set](image/163a1239ffb34f7e.webp)

> ❗Note: This operation erases all links between the mind map and the document, and it may be impossible to undo.
> However, it does not delete the document from MarginNote. You can restore a document that has been closed from a study set but not deleted from the document library.

### 3.3 Reordering Tabs

On the tab management screen, tap `Reorder` in the top-right corner to change the display order of the tabs:

After tapping `Reorder`, press and hold the "three-line handle" to freely drag the document `tabs` into the order you want. When you are done, tap `Done`.

![Reordering documents, step 1](image/643a23cb491ac2b1.webp)

![Reordering documents, step 2](image/11c3ea6e7086bd53.webp)

### 3.4 Managing the Number of Tabs Shown

Sometimes a study set contains a large number of documents, which can be hard to manage. In that case, on the tab management screen under `Documents in Tabs`, set the `Maximum Number of Tabs` to control how many documents the tab bar shows. Documents beyond the limit are hidden but remain in the `study set`.

> 💡We recommend a setting of 3-7, adjusted to your screen size and the number of documents.

![Managing the number of tabs shown](image/363278b879d9d95b.webp)
