## 2. The Handwriting Toolbar

After you enable handwriting mode in a document, mind map, card, or review scenario, the handwriting toolbar pops up automatically, supporting custom configuration and operations.

- Tap different icons on the `handwriting toolbar` to switch between pen modes
- Tap an icon again to open the handwriting customization popup, where you can change the pen's color, type, thickness, opacity, and more

![Handwriting customization popup](image/f68d2b5f0197d698.webp)

### 2.1. Moving the Handwriting Toolbar

You can drag the handwriting toolbar to a convenient spot. It snaps automatically to the edge of the screen, a document, or a card, as shown:

> 💡If the handwriting toolbar has too many pens, it may not be able to snap to the top, because space there is limited

![Animated demo: Moving and snapping the handwriting toolbar](image/6a977b131721013f.webp)

- Tap a **different pen-shaped icon** to switch pen tools

- After tapping a pen tool, you'll see the color bar at the top of the handwriting customization popup, made up of 11 color icons and one color-picker icon

![](image/f590aa06109a64f6.webp)

#### 2.2.3. Customizing the Color Bar

Color picker

Tap the color picker (shown by the icon above) to customize a color. You can also import a style to reuse a ready-made color palette with one tap.

**Method 1: Change via RGB values and Hex codes**

Tap the color-picker icon to open the color configuration popup. Enter a value from 0-255 in each of the R/G/B fields, or enter "# + six-digit code" in the HEX field (e.g., #FF0000), to customize the color.

![Animated demo: Changing color via RGB values and Hex codes](image/29d8be573f59e07c.webp)

**Method 2: Pick a color from the color disc**

> 💡When you're not sure of the exact RGB values you want, you can pick a color directly from the color disc.

- The color configuration popup contains a color display area and two color discs:
  - Color display area: the first two cells are pure white / pure black, the third cell is the original color, and the fourth cell is the adjusted new color;
  - Color discs: the upper disc adjusts **brightness and saturation**, and the lower disc **adjusts hue**;
- How to use: tap a disc to pick a color, or slide your pen tip to adjust in real time; compare with the original color and release to confirm

![Animated demo: Picking a color from the color disc](image/4b376c46df1665da.webp)

#### 2.2.6. Duplicating and Deleting Pens

> 💡**When you add a new pen, you may just want a different color**:
> 
> MN4 lets you duplicate a pen tool directly, so parameters you've already set (such as thickness and opacity) don't need to be configured again

You can configure the `handwriting toolbar` faster by duplicating and deleting pens.

In the current version, the handwriting toolbar can hold at most 12 brushes and at least 3 brushes (one Pen, one Highlighter, and one Pencil)  

> 💡Max users can configure up to 12 brushes; Pro users can configure up to 8 brushes

- Select a pen tool and tap it again to bring up the handwriting customization popup, then tap `Clone As New Tool`; the new tool has exactly the same configuration as the original.

##### 2.2.6.2. Deleting a Pen

Tap the pen tool you want to delete, tap it again to bring up the handwriting customization popup, then tap Delete; the pen is removed from the toolbar

> 💡Deleting a pen tool cannot be undone, so proceed with caution

### 2.3. Auxiliary Tools

#### 2.3.1. Ruler

- Select a pen tool and tap it again to see the `Ruler` switch in the handwriting customization popup
- Turn on the switch to summon the ruler tool in that module (mind map, document, or card)

##### 2.3.1.1. Adjusting the Ruler's Position and Angle

- Tap the `Ruler` and drag to move it to where you want
- Press the `Ruler` with **two fingers** and rotate to adjust its angle.

> 💡If the ruler is enabled in the mind map, document, and card, you can adjust each one separately.
> 
> If tapping does nothing, **first make a stroke in that area with your pen** so that MN recognizes which ruler tool you're currently using

Draw along the ruler with your pen tip and a straight line is generated automatically, showing the value at the pen tip's position.

### 2.4. Eraser Tool

#### 2.4.1. Eraser Settings

Eraser

After opening the handwriting toolbar, select the eraser tool (shown above) to erase handwriting strokes with the `Eraser`

Tap the `Eraser` tool again to bring up the eraser settings popup

1. **Complete Erase vs. Incomplete Erase**

> 💡When erasing with the eraser tool, sometimes you need to erase an entire stroke, such as when deleting large sections of notes.
> 
> Other times you only need to erase part of a stroke, such as when modifying a drawing.

In the eraser settings popup, set the `Eraser` state to `Complete`, and the eraser will erase **every part of any stroke it touches**.

Switch the `Eraser` type to `Incomplete`, and the eraser will only erase **the pixels it actually touches**.

![Animated demo: Eraser - Complete](image/14c049094658610b.webp)

![Animated demo: Eraser - Incomplete](image/681f9d6f287eeeae.webp)

1. **Auto-cancel**

In the eraser settings popup, turn on the `Auto-cancel` switch, and after you erase once with the `Eraser`, it will **automatically cancel the eraser tool** and switch back to the last pen tool you used.

> 💡With Auto-cancel on, as soon as you stop erasing, the eraser immediately turns back into the handwriting tool you were using, simplifying the workflow to "**select the eraser tool → erase a part → keep writing**" and avoiding the hassle of picking the wrong tool when manually switching back

![Animated demo: Eraser auto-cancel](image/e1fcba36ea4f1b20.webp)

#### 2.4.2. Scribble Erase

**You can erase with just the pen, without switching to the eraser:**

Step 1: Turn on `Perfect Shape` for any pen

Step 2: Scribble over your notes with that pen, then hold the pen tip on the screen for about 0.5 seconds to erase the handwriting strokes

Lasso

After opening the handwriting toolbar, select the `lasso tool` (shown above). Touch icon

> 💡When frequently switching between actions like writing and excerpting, you can use this tool to reduce switching between functions

## 3. Sketch Layers

Sketch layers are the "layers" of documents and mind maps. You can write on different "layers," and they support operations such as stacking multiple layers and switching between them. Both documents and mind maps have the sketch layer feature, and they work in similar ways.

> 💡The `sketch layer` feature makes MarginNote more efficient and convenient for drilling problems, reviewing, and organizing notes. It removes any limit on how many times you can redo problems: you can create multiple sketch layers to practice repeatedly, and the layer-stacking concept makes side-by-side comparison more efficient, helping you summarize and review.
> 
> For example, when drilling problems for review, you can create a new layer for each round and name it with the date, such as "**Functions round1**". You can also hide past strokes to focus on the current round, or stack different sketch layers semi-transparently to compare differences in problem-solving approaches.

1. **Creating a Sketch Layer**:

When no sketch layer has been added, your handwriting is bound to the "Default" layer. Tap the "＋" icon to bring up the `sketch layer naming popup`.

By default, sketch layers are named "Practice Sheet 1, Practice Sheet 2……"

1. **Other Sketch Layer Operations**:

On the handwriting settings page, tap "`Edit`" in the top-right corner to enter the `sketch layer editing page`:

- Tap the "..." icon to the right of a sketch layer, then tap `Delete` to delete that sketch layer

> ⚠️Once you choose to delete, the handwriting in that sketch layer, any bound text boxes, and so on will all disappear, and the undo action will not work, so proceed with caution.

> For more settings, see: [Layers: Document Notebooks & Sketch Layers](../6gbzKZ4a16uYGaFNLMYASD/index.md)
