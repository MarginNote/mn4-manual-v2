# Creating Text Boxes, Images, and Photos

> 💡`Text box` - **Annotate in place**: Jot down supplements, key points, and questions right beside the original text, so your ideas don't end up scattered elsewhere.
> 
> `Image`\*\* **-**Illustrate a process \*\*: Insert screenshots or diagrams that you can resize, copy, cut, or delete for clearer information.
> 
> `Take Photo`-**Instant capture for classes, meetings, and book study**: Snap pages, whiteboards, and other materials on the spot and save them directly as document images or mind map cards.
> 
> Bring your own thoughts, images, and real-time content together on a single page for more efficient reading, faster review, and clearer sharing.

## 1 Adding a Text Box in a Document

Text box

![Text box icon Text box icon ](image/ba8f37e14d3f5999.webp)

There are three ways to add a `Text box`:

- Method 1: Click the `Text box` icon in the document's top navigation bar, click where you want to insert the `Text box`, and type your text in the `Text box` that pops up.

![Click the Text box icon in the document's top navigation bar, click where you want to insert the Text box, and type your text in the Text box that pops up. Click the Text box icon in the document's top navigation bar, click where you want to insert the Text box, and type your text in the Text box that pops up. ](image/18940a9effcfb79d.webp)

- Method 2: Long-press or right-click an empty area of the document to bring up the pop-up menu, then tap the `Text box` icon.

![](image/4d93188f544ec913.webp)

![In More - Customize, you can select the icons you want to add to the top-level menu. In More - Customize, you can select the icons you want to add to the top-level menu. ](image/d40c44da533c1cb2.webp)

- Method 3: Drag content in directly from another app. With Split View on iPad or multiple windows on Mac, you can drag text from another app straight into a MarginNote document to automatically create a `Text box`.

## 2 Adding Images in Documents and Mind Maps

### 2.1 Adding Images in a Document

Image

There are three ways to add an image:

- Method 1: **The simplest** - Drag in directly from another app: With Split View on iPad or multiple windows on Mac, you can drag images from another app straight into a MarginNote document.
- Method 2: Click the `Image` button in the document's top navigation bar (as shown by the icon above), click where you want it, and then choose an image from your local photo library to insert it.

![](image/c9b8ff7f34224d06.webp)

- Method 3: Long-press or right-click an empty area of the document to bring up the pop-up menu, then tap the `Image` button.

![](image/a6d9b0b5cacac3f1.webp)

![In More - Customize, you can select the icons you want to add to the top-level menu. In More - Customize, you can select the icons you want to add to the top-level menu. ](image/2c05b61f8d006f1c.webp)

> 💡On the iPad version, after adding an image you need to deselect the image icon and switch to the hand tool - Document or another function in the `document top navigation bar` before you can long-press to bring up the pop-up menu again;
> On the Mac version, this does not affect right-clicking to bring up the pop-up menu.

#### 2.1.1 Working with Images

Click an inserted image to resize, copy, cut, or delete it.

![](image/469fd1309f0e7f2b.webp)

##### 2.1.1.1 Resizing an Image

Drag the blue triangle in the bottom-right corner of the image to change its size.

##### 2.1.1.2 Copy

Copy an image so you can paste it elsewhere.

![Copy and then paste to get two identical images. Copy and then paste to get two identical images. ](image/016403e5e5dcdd80.webp)

##### 2.1.1.3 Cut

After you cut an image and paste it again in the document, the image moves from its original spot to the new location; if you add it to a mind map, it is saved as a new card.

### 2.2 Adding Images as Cards in a Mind Map

There are two ways to add an `Image` in a mind map:

- Method 1: **The simplest** - Drag in directly from another app: With Split View on iPad or multiple windows on Mac, you can drag images from another app straight into a MarginNote mind map.
- Method 2: Paste an image from the system clipboard directly into the mind map (Command+V) to create a new card containing the image in the mind map.

![](image/c7ea8f45855b5e1e.webp)

## 3 Taking Photos in Documents and Mind Maps

> 💡The camera in MarginNote automatically uses a **continuous scanner** to capture the useful content in view; learners can also tap the shutter button to take a photo themselves.

### 3.1 Taking a Photo in a Document and Inserting It as an Image

Camera

Long-press or right-click an empty area of the document to bring up the pop-up menu, then tap the `Camera` button (as shown by the icon above) to take a photo; working with the photo is the same as working with an `Image` in a document, described above.

![](image/edff9de933acad62.webp)

![In Tools - Customize, you can select the icons you want to add to the top-level menu. In Tools - Customize, you can select the icons you want to add to the top-level menu. ](image/4c704aa8008424c7.webp)

### 3.2 Taking a Photo in a Mind Map and Saving It as a Card

1. Click an empty area of the mind map to bring up the pop-up menu, then tap the `Camera` icon to take a photo.

![](image/c7ea8f45855b5e1e.webp)

1. After you take photos, a single photo or multiple photos are saved as one card.

![](image/d73d1f3520eb7347.webp)

1. Each photo becomes a comment, and you can rearrange the photos in the card editor.

   ![](image/7fa13cc754fe2ba2.webp)
